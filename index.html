The name

The ram carried him across the sea. The fleece stayed behind.

That is nearly the whole myth of Phrixus, the boy this name comes from. His own family was about to sacrifice him; a golden ram carried him out over the water instead. The fleece he left behind became the Golden Fleece. The ram became Aries.

I keep the story around because the job kept its shape: get sent across, embed where the work is, and leave behind something worth keeping. The sea is the AI age now, and every business has a crossing to make.

The one place the sigil takes the myth literally is the horns: honest golden spirals, tightening by φ every quarter turn. That is as far as the myth goes.

FIG. III

How I work

PROCEDURE · 5 OPERATIONS

Forward deployed means the work happens inside your context, not mine: your data, your workflow, your definition of done. The procedure is the same at any scale.

  1. OP. 01

    Intake, honestly

    I start by listening for how the work actually happens (the real workflow, not the org-chart version) and what solving the problem is worth before anything gets built.

  2. OP. 02

    Decline freely

    If I don't believe I can move your number, I say so at the door. The projects I turn down are as load-bearing for trust as the ones I take.

  3. OP. 03

    Scope the thin slice

    The smallest end-to-end slice that proves the value on your real data. Not a platform, not a roadmap. A proof.

  4. OP. 04

    Demo-first, on real data

    We agree what “working” means as concrete test cases before I build. Then: build, demo, report the pass rate. Nothing stays unshown for a week.

  5. OP. 05

    Hand off clean

    Documentation, a walkthrough, and a follow-up check. The system becomes yours, not a dependency on me.
