How this site
was built

Designed and built end-to-end by Claude (Fable 5, Anthropic) in one autonomous session: concept, identity, code, copy, and the assets around it. Every line of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript is hand-written; no site builders, no templates, no frameworks.

The identity

Frixos is the Greek name of Phrixus, the boy who escaped on the back of a golden ram: the ram whose fleece became the Golden Fleece and who was set among the stars as Aries. The sigil takes the myth literally in one respect only: the horns are true golden spirals, r = r₀·φ^(−2θ/π), φ growth per quarter turn. The "golden" in golden ram is mathematics, not costume. The mark is generated parametrically (see tools/export-brand.mjs and assets/js/main.js; the same geometry draws the favicon, the nav mark, the hero construction drawing, and the constellation).

The concept bake-off

Three genuinely different directions were designed and built as single-fold prototypes, screenshotted at desktop and mobile widths, and judged against the brief: signal senior judgment and shipping ability to hiring managers and prospective clients. The losers live on under /concepts/a-aries and /concepts/c-crossing.

A · "Aries, the Instrument" (runner-up)

A dark celestial chart: ink-indigo, graticule arcs, mono coordinates, the sigil assembling as a gold constellation. Elegant and thematically rich, but the closest of the three to the default "dark portfolio with an accent color," and the constellation figure was ambiguous at a glance. It lost the site but won a section: FIG. II is its constellation, given a home as one dark plate in a light drawing set.

B · "The Drawing" (winner)

The myth, engineered: gallery-paper ground, a massive grotesque wordmark, and the sigil presented as a live technical construction drawing with real φ annotations. It won because it is the only concept where the identity itself is the hero, it extends naturally into a whole site (a numbered drawing set with evidence tables, procedure plates, a title block), and it performs the positioning instead of decorating it: measured, documented, evidence-first.

C · "The Crossing"

A raw-WebGL fragment shader of the night sea with a gold light-path: Phrixus's crossing. The single most cinematic image of the three, and the first to be cut: the identity almost disappears (no sigil, no system), the metaphor doesn't extend past the fold, and on a narrow phone it is mostly empty water. Spectacle lost to restraint, as it should here.

Technique inventory

  • Parametric identity: one golden-spiral definition generates every rendering of the mark: solid, construction drawing, constellation, favicon.
  • Live construction drawing: the hero sigil draws its own spiral centerlines (SVG stroke-dash animation), blooms gold, then lets its construction geometry recede.
  • Constellation plate: the same spiral sampled as stars, line-drawn on scroll into view.
  • Type system: Cabinet Grotesk (display), Switzer (text), Spline Sans Mono (annotations); variable fonts, self-hosted.
  • Progressive enhancement: entrance animations are gated behind a JS-added class; without JavaScript (or with prefers-reduced-motion) nothing is ever hidden and the drawing renders complete.
  • Evidence as design: the Sentys benchmark table is set like a test record, because the numbers are real, and real numbers deserve a title block.

Generated media (disclosed)

Three AI-generated assets ship on this site, all made with Higgsfield models and disclosed in place: the 4K upscale of the owner's portrait (Bytedance upscaler over the original photograph), the golden-fleece macro still (Soul 2.0), and the four-second fleece shimmer clip (Seedance 1.5, compressed to ~380 KB). Everything else (sigil, charts, layouts, OG image, LinkedIn banner) is hand-built SVG, CSS, and code, including the static canvas starfield behind the constellation.
